## Team Analysis: Seattle Kraken

The Kraken enter 2026 with a renewed sense of purpose after a solid playoff push in 2025. Under head coach Dan Bylsma (assuming continuity), Seattle's identity revolves around a balanced attack and stout defense. Key strengths include:

- **Offensive Firepower**: Led by Jared McCann and Chandler Stephenson, the Kraken boast a top-10 power-play unit in the league. They excel at generating high-danger chances from the slot, making them a threat against teams with weaker penalty kills.
  
- **Defensive Stability**: Goaltender Philipp Grubauer has been a rock, posting a .915 save percentage last season. The defensive corps, anchored by Vince Dunn and Adam Larsson, prioritizes shot suppression, though they can be vulnerable to fast transitions.

- **Recent Form**: Seattle has gone 7-3-0 in their last 10 games, with strong road performances. However, injuries to depth forwards could test their secondary scoring.

Weaknesses? The Kraken's even-strength goal differential has been mediocre, and they struggle against teams that control the neutral zone. Against the Devils' speedy wingers, Seattle will need to tighten up their breakout.

## Team Analysis: New Jersey Devils

The Devils are shaping up as Eastern Conference contenders in 2026, building on their 2024-2025 resurgence. Coach Sheldon Keefe's system emphasizes speed, creativity, and relentless pressure. Here's what makes them tick:

- **Star Power**: Jack Hughes remains the engine, with elite vision and playmaking. Paired with Nico Hischier's two-way excellence and Jesper Bratt's scoring touch, New Jersey's top line is among the league's most dangerous. Defenseman Luke Hughes adds offensive pop from the blue line.

- **Goaltending and Structure**: If Akira Schmid continues his development, the Devils could have a reliable last line of defense. Their forecheck is aggressive, forcing turnovers that lead to odd-man rushes—a nightmare for teams like Seattle that like to set up in the offensive zone.

- **Recent Form**: The Devils have been inconsistent on the road but dominant at home (8-2-0 record in Newark). They rank top-5 in shots per game but can be hampered by defensive lapses, allowing too many high-quality chances.

Challenges for New Jersey include depth beyond their top six forwards and occasional power-play slumps. If Seattle clamps down on transition play, the Devils' offense might stall.
