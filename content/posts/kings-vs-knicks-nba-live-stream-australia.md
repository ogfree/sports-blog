## When and Where to Watch: Game Schedules for Australian Time Zones

The NBA season typically runs from October to April, with the Kings and Knicks facing off multiple times. For the 2025-2026 season, expect games to start in the early hours Australian Eastern Standard Time (AEST) due to the US time difference—often between 11:00 AM and 2:00 PM AEST for East Coast games.

### Key Schedules to Note
- **Check Official Sources**: Always verify exact dates on the NBA website (nba.com) or apps like the NBA App. Game times shift slightly year-to-year.
- **Season Highlights**: Look out for rivalry games around Christmas or All-Star Weekend—prime viewing times for Australians.
- **Time Zone Tip**: Use apps like World Time Buddy to convert ET to AEST. For example, a 7:00 PM ET tip-off in New York translates to around 11:00 AM AEST the next day in Sydney.

Pro tip: Set reminders on your phone calendar for upcoming matchups to avoid spoilers from social media.

## Technical Setup: Ensuring Smooth Streaming in Australia

Australian internet speeds can vary, especially in regional areas. Here's how to optimize:

### Internet and Device Requirements
- **Speed**: Aim for at least 10Mbps for HD streaming; 25Mbps for 4K. Test your speed at speedtest.net.
- **Devices**: Compatible with iOS 12+, Android 8+, smart TVs (Samsung, LG), or streaming sticks (Amazon Fire TV, Roku).
- **VPN Use**: If traveling or facing blocks, use a VPN with Australian servers. NordVPN or Surfshark work well for NBA apps.

### Troubleshooting Tips
- **Buffering?** Switch to a wired Ethernet connection or reduce video quality in app settings.
- **Geo-Blocks**: NBA League Pass is seamless in Australia, but if issues arise, contact support.
- **Data Limits**: Mobile users—stream on Wi-Fi to avoid high data usage.

### Accessibility Features
