Match day setup: Avoid buffering and enjoy smooth streaming

Internet speed guidelines
- SD (480p): Minimum 3–5 Mbps
- HD (720p/1080p): 10–15 Mbps
- 4K (if available): 25+ Mbps

Actionable tips
- Choose “Auto” or “Medium” quality if your data is inconsistent; avoid 4K on mobile networks.
- Use 5GHz Wi-Fi for lower latency (if router is close); 2.4GHz has better range but more interference.
- Close background apps, pause cloud backups/downloads during the match.
- Prefer Ethernet for laptops/desktops or a wired connection for streaming boxes.
- Update the app and OS before kickoff to avoid last-minute glitches.
- Keep a backup: If one platform struggles, switch to the broadcaster’s secondary feed or language stream.

TV-specific tips
- If your DTH signal is weak during rain, switch to OTT streaming.
- For smart TVs, disable “motion smoothing” for natural motion; set picture mode to “Movie” or “Sports” depending on preference.
- Use a streaming stick (Fire TV, Chromecast, Roku) if the built-in TV app is slow.
