## Tactical Analysis

### Club Brugge: Control and Precision
Brugge typically sets up in a fluid **4-3-3** or **3-5-2** formation. Their game plan revolves around controlling possession in the midfield third and utilizing the width provided by their wing-backs.

*   **The Build-up:** Brugge’s center-backs split wide, allowing the defensive midfielder to drop deep and dictate the tempo.
*   **The Weakness:** Occasionally vulnerable to counter-attacks against pacy wingers, though their recovery speed is generally excellent.

### Union Saint-Gilloise: Chaos and Pressing
USG favors a **3-4-1-2** system that relies on overwhelming the opponent in the middle of the pitch. They do not allow opponents time on the ball; instead, they force mistakes high up the pitch.

*   **The Strategy:** They will look to disrupt Brugge’s rhythm early. If they can cut off passing lanes to Brugge’s creative midfielders, they can turn the game into a physical battle.
*   **The Weakness:** Their high defensive line can be exposed by precise long balls over the top, a tactic Brugge employs frequently.
