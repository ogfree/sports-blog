# World Baseball Classic 2026: Schedule, Teams & How to Watch Guide

The World Baseball Classic (WBC) is gearing up for its next iteration in 2026, promising a high-stakes tournament featuring the world's best players competing for national pride. As the premier international baseball tournament, the WBC brings together elite talent from Major League Baseball (MLB) and leagues around the globe.

This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about the World Baseball Classic 2026, including the tournament schedule, participating teams, and how to watch the action live.

## What is the World Baseball Classic?

The World Baseball Classic is an international baseball tournament sanctioned by the World Baseball Softball Confederation (WBSC). First held in 2006, the WBC takes place every four years, featuring national teams from across the world. The tournament is known for its intense atmosphere, passionate fanbases, and the rare opportunity to see MLB superstars representing their home countries.

Japan is the reigning champion, having won the 2023 tournament, and will look to defend their title in 2026.

## World Baseball Classic 2026 Schedule

While the official schedule for the 2026 World Baseball Classic has not been fully finalized by the MLB and WBSC, the tournament typically follows a standard format. The event is expected to take place in **March 2026**.

The tournament generally consists of several stages:

*   **Pool Play:** Teams are divided into groups (typically four pools) and play a round-robin format. The top teams from each pool advance to the knockout stage.
*   **Quarterfinals:** The top teams from the pools face off in single-elimination games.
*   **Semifinals:** The winners of the quarterfinals compete for a spot in the championship.
*   **Championship Game:** The final two teams battle for the WBC title.

## How to Watch the World Baseball Classic 2026

Accessing the games depends on your location and broadcasting rights. As the tournament approaches, these details will be finalized. Here is the expected breakdown based on previous tournaments:

### In the United States
*   **Broadcast Partner:** The tournament is typically broadcast on **ESPN** networks (ESPN, ESPN2, ABC). Games are often aired in English and Spanish.
*   **Streaming:** Games are usually available via **ESPN+** or the **ESPN App** for subscribers. Additionally, MLB.TV may offer packages for international viewers.

### International Viewing
*   **Japan:** Games are commonly broadcast on **NHK**, **TBS**, and **TV Asahi**.
*   **Latin America:** Viewers can typically watch on **ESPN Latin America** and **MLB Network**.
*   **Other Regions:** Local sports networks in participating countries usually hold the rights. Check your local sports listings for availability.

### Free Streaming Options
In some regions, the WBC may be available on free-to-air television or via official streaming platforms on YouTube or social media channels. Follow the official MLB and WBC social media accounts for updates on free streaming options.
