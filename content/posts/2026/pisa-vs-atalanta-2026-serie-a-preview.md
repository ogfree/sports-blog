## Tactical Analysis

### Pisa’s Strategy: Defensive Resilience
Pisa’s survival hopes hinge on their ability to frustrate Atalanta. Expect a deep defensive block with two banks of four. Their game plan will be to exploit Atalanta’s high defensive line using the pace of their wing-backs, particularly Birindelli, on the counter-attack. Set pieces will be vital; with Lucca’s height, corners could be their best route to goal.

### Atalanta’s Approach: Possession and Pressing
Atalanta is renowned for their "Gasotel" style—high pressing and rapid transitions. Without the burden of European semi-finals this late in May, they should play with high energy. The wing-backs, Ruggeri and Zappacosta, will look to stretch Pisa’s narrow defense, creating gaps for Retegui to exploit centrally.

### Key Battles to Watch
1.  **Lucca vs. Hien:** The physical battle between Pisa’s target man and Atalanta’s agile defender will dictate the aerial duels.
2.  **Birindelli vs. Zappacosta:** The flank battle. If Pisa’s right wing-back can hold his ground defensively, Atalanta’s left-side attack may be neutralized.
