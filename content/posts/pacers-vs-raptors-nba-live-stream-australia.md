## Official Streaming Options in Australia

As of 2026, the broadcasting landscape for the NBA in Australia remains robust, with a few key players holding the rights.

### 1. Kayo Sports (Foxtel)
Kayo Sports is arguably the most popular streaming service for Australian sports fans. It holds the rights to broadcast NBA games live.
*   **Pros:** No lock-in contracts, HD streaming, and features like SplitView (watch two games at once).
*   **Cons:** Requires a subscription fee.

### 2. NBA League Pass
The official streaming service of the NBA is available directly to Australian subscribers.
*   **Pros:** Access to every single game (subject to blackout restrictions), Condensed Games replays, and access to NBA TV content.
*   **Cons:** Can be more expensive than local sports packages, and blackout restrictions may apply if a local network picks up the game (though less common for Pacers/Raptors matchups in Australia).

### 3. Foxtel (Cable and Foxtel Now)
If you prefer traditional cable or the Foxtel Now app, the ESPN channel (which broadcasts NBA games) is included in specific packs.
*   **Pros:** Reliable broadcast quality.
*   **Cons:** Requires a set-top box for cable or a specific subscription tier for Foxtel Now.

### 4. Free-to-Air (Channel 10)

*   **Update for 2026:** Check the local TV guide or the 10play app. While marquee games like the Lakers or Warriors are prioritized, occasional Pacers or Raptors games might be broadcast depending on scheduling.
